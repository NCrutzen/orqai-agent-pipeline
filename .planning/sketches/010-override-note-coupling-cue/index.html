Sketch 010 override-note-coupling-cue · phase 82.5

Q: When the override picker opens, how do we make it obvious the textarea note attaches?

Variant A — Tooltip arrow

When the override picker opens, a small tooltip pops out pointing from the picker to the textarea: "Note will be attached." The arrow visually wires the two surfaces together.

Trade-off: overlays add visual noise; tooltips on already-busy detail panes risk obscuring other content.

Discoverability: new operators see it once and learn the coupling. Dismissable after first appearance.

Variant B — Shared accent border

When the picker is focused, both the picker AND the textarea get a shared amber glow. The shared color treatment communicates "these belong together." A small "⤓ Note attaches" label floats under the picker.

Subtler than a tooltip. Works in muscle memory once learned — the glow IS the cue, no microcopy needed.

Risk: focus rings already mean "active". Operators may not parse the duplicated treatment as a coupling signal.

Variant C — Inline microcopy

Plain microcopy under the picker: "⤴ Will be attached to the override" lives on the textarea, persistent. The picker stays untouched.

Cheapest to build. No animation, no focus tricks. Operator reads the line once and knows.

Trade-off: persistent text adds visual chatter for veteran operators who don't need the reminder.
