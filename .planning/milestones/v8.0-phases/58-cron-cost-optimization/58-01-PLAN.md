<objective>
Bring Inngest free-tier usage back under 50k runs/month by windowing four high-frequency crons to business-hours-only operation. Width is 06:00–19:58 Europe/Amsterdam, Mon–Fri (covers the requested 06:30 → 19:30 envelope plus 30-min slop on each side, since standard cron cannot express :30 boundaries cleanly within `*/2` cadence).

Side effect: drops Supabase realtime fan-out (the bridge cron is the single biggest writer to tables that `postgres_changes` listeners watch).

Out of scope: refactoring the realtime provider's 4× `postgres_changes` subscriptions into broadcast — separate phase, higher risk, lower leverage now that the bridge cadence is halved.
</objective>

<context>
Two billing alerts triggered this phase:

1. **Inngest** — 42,074/50,000 free-tier runs, mid-month.
2. **Supabase** — 6,475,647 realtime messages (cap 5,500,000); grace until 2026-05-26.

|---|---|---|

| (rest, unchanged) | | ~2,602 |
| **Total** | | **~34,018** |

That's a 65% reduction, fitting the free tier with ~16k headroom for event-driven runs (pipeline, triage, worker shards).

Trade-offs accepted:
- **Bridge cadence**: 1 min → 2 min within the window. V7 dashboard sync latency goes from ≤1 min to ≤2 min.
- **No overnight processing**: emails arriving after 19:30 sit in Outlook until next business day's 06:00 tick. iController cleanup dispatcher behaves the same. Acceptable per ops practice.
- **Weekends**: weekend traffic (rare for accounting workflows) processes Monday 06:00. orqai-trace-sync needs lookback widened from 15 min → 72 h so Mon-morning tick covers Sun+weekend.
- **Browserless cold starts**: outside business hours, the first weekday-morning automation may pay one cold-start penalty (~5 s). Acceptable — no human is waiting at 04:00.

Cron-string note: `*/2 6-19 * * 1-5` fires at minute 0,2,4,…,58 of hours 6 through 19 inclusive. That's 06:00→19:58, slightly wider than the requested 06:30→19:30 because cron's `*/2` step doesn't reset at :30. Net cost difference: ~30 ticks/day = trivial.
</context>
