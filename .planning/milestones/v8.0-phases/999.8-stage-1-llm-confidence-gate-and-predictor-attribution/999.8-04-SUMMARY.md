## Task 2 — Operator review of proposed prompt diff (BLOCKING CHECKPOINT)

### Proposed prompt addendum

Insert the following block near the **top** of the agent's `instructions`, **above** any existing few-shots, immediately after the role/task framing:

```xml
<confidence_calibration>
Choose confidence using THIS calibration:

- "high": You are ~95%+ certain this email matches the chosen noise category.
  The signals MUST be unambiguous. If a reasonable human reviewer would need
  to read more than the subject + first 200 chars of body to agree with you,
  the confidence is NOT high. Examples that qualify: machine-generated
  out-of-office replies with explicit return dates; structured bank-system
  payment confirmations with reference numbers; ticketing-system
  auto-acknowledgements with case IDs.

- "medium": Signals present but not overwhelming. A reasonable reviewer
  could read the email and disagree with your category. THE DEFAULT FOR ANY
  AMBIGUITY IS MEDIUM, not high. Examples: a forwarded invoice that mentions
  payment but also asks for a credit note; an OOO phrase buried mid-thread
  in a substantive reply; a payment-confirmation-shaped email that lacks
  the structured template of a bank system.

- "low": You are guessing — the email is unclear and you would not be
  surprised to be wrong. Better to admit low confidence than to anchor on a
  weak signal.

ASYMMETRIC COST: a wrong "high" prediction auto-archives the email and may
cause downstream financial damage (e.g. an invoice-correction request
labeled "Payment Admittance" and removed from inbox before any human sees
it). A "medium" or "low" prediction is queued for a human reviewer at zero
business cost. WHEN IN DOUBT, DOWNGRADE.
</confidence_calibration>
```
