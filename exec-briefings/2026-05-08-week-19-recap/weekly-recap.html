Section 1 · How it works now
Same process, every brand, every team
Every customer email goes through five steps.
Until last week, each brand and each team had its own custom script. Now there's one shared process — the same five steps run for Smeba, Berki, Fire Control, every other brand, and (next week) Sales. Adding the next mailbox is configuration, not a rebuild.
The five-step process · live in production
Each step does one job. If it can't decide, the email goes to a human queue with the reason flagged.
Step 1

Security check

Spots manipulation attempts and limits cost per email before any AI reads the message.
This week: hardened so the inbox keeps moving even if the AI hangs.
Step 2

Sort the noise

Auto-replies, out-of-office, payment confirmations are archived without a human ever seeing them.
This week: added a smart sorter (Cleo) for everything the simple rules can't slot.
Step 3

Match the customer

Figures out which customer the email is from — across 6 brands and 4 languages.
This week: the match is now automatically tagged in iController. No more manual tagging.
Step 4

Decide the action

Reads the email and routes it to the right specialist — invoice copy, payment dispute, address change, etc.
This week: a team lead (Debbie) now splits emails when one message needs more than one specialist.
Step 5

Do the work

A specialist drafts the reply, attaches the right file, queues it in iController. Operator hits Send.
This week: the same specialists now work for any team. Sales reuses the Debtor specialists out-of-the-box.
Shipped

One unified process for every brand

Replaced the patchwork of brand-specific scripts. Every email — Smeba, Berki, Fire Control, Sicli, Smeba Fire — now follows the same five steps. Adding a new brand is configuration, not a rebuild.

Shipped

Onboarding a new mailbox = a config row

The platform's "what does each mailbox do" map moved from hard-coded into the codebase to a configuration table. Future mailboxes (UK, IE, new departments) onboard by adding a row, not by writing code.

Shipped

One audit trail for every AI decision

Every choice the AI makes — and every operator override — now lands in one place. Full transparency, ready for compliance review, and the foundation for measuring impact per intent.

Shipped

Sales mailbox can clean its own noise

When Sales goes live next week, the AI can already auto-archive auto-replies and out-of-office messages. Sales reuses the Debtor pipeline end-to-end, including SugarCRM where Debtor uses iController.

Shipped

Operator queue: nothing falls through

If the AI isn't confident, the email shows up in a human queue with the reason flagged: "low confidence", "no specialist exists yet", or "specialist failed". Operators always know what to look at.

Shipped

Security layer is now bulletproof

The Step 1 security check now fails gracefully. If the AI hangs or hiccups, emails keep flowing. Operators are never blocked by a backend issue — the platform self-heals and sweeps stale rows.
